History of NRL

The National Rugby League (NRL) is the top league of professional rugby league clubs in Australasia. The NRL was formed in the aftermath of the 1990s' Super League war as a joint partnership between the Australian Rugby League (ARL) and News Corporation-controlled Super League, after both organizations ran parallel competitions in 1997.

Teams

The NRL consists of 16 teams: 15 based in Australia and one based in New Zealand. These teams compete in the premiership season from March to October, culminating in the NRL Grand Final, which is one of Australia's most popular sporting events.

Rules of the Game

Rugby league is a full-contact sport played by two teams of thirteen players on a rectangular field. Points are scored by carrying or kicking the ball to the opposing team's end zone. The main ways to score are through tries, goals, and field goals.

Famous Players

The NRL has seen many legendary players over the years, including names like Andrew Johns, Darren Lockyer, and Cameron Smith. These players have made significant contributions to the sport and have become icons in the rugby league community.
